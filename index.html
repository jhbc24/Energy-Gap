Renewable Energies

Solar Energy

Solar energy is the most abundant, renewable, and sustainable energy source derived from the sun's rays. It is harnessed using solar panels and converted into electricity or heat for residential, commercial, and industrial applications.

Wind Energy

Wind energy is captured using wind turbines to convert the kinetic energy from wind into mechanical power. This clean, renewable resource helps generate electricity without emitting greenhouse gases.

Hydro Energy

Hydro energy, or hydropower, utilizes the movement of water, such as rivers or dams, to produce electricity. It is a reliable and consistent source of renewable energy that contributes to grid stability.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy is derived from the heat stored beneath the Earth's surface. This heat can be used for electricity generation, heating, and cooling applications, providing a sustainable and reliable energy source.

Biomass Energy

Biomass energy comes from organic materials such as plant and animal waste. These materials are converted into biofuels or used directly for heating and electricity generation, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
